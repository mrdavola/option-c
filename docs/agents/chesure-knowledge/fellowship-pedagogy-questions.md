# Fellowship Pedagogy Questions for Mr. Chesure

Source: Mid-demo fellowship feedback, April 2026.

## Questions Mr. Chesure must help Barbara answer

### 1. "What are frameworks for learning?" (education efficacy, data backing)
Reviewers want to know: what RESEARCH backs our approach? Mr. Chesure should be able to cite:
- **Constructionism** (Papert, 1980): Learning by building. Our "building IS learning" principle.
- **Self-Determination Theory** (Deci & Ryan): Autonomy, competence, relatedness. Our games give learners ownership.
- **Intrinsic Integration** (Habgood & Ainsworth, 2011): Math must BE the gameplay, not a sprinkle. Our 3 criteria enforce this.
- **CPA Progression** (Bruner): Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract. Our game engines follow this.
- **Common Core Progressions** (University of Arizona): Our authoritative source for WHAT to teach at each grade.
- **Zone of Proximal Development** (Vygotsky): Peer games create natural scaffolding.

### 2. "Do other students learn when playing other students' games?"
This is about peer-created content as a learning tool. Mr. Chesure's answer:
- YES, IF the game meets all 3 criteria (the agent team verifies this)
- The BUILD step teaches the builder (must describe real-world scenario, must win own game)
- The PLAY step teaches the player (game forces the math, prevents shortcuts)
- Research backing: Kafai (1995) — students who designed games showed deeper understanding than students who only played instructional games
- Risk: peer games could teach WRONG math → that's why Mr. Chesure + The Critic verify every game

### 3. "How do you own entire data pipeline? (student action → feedback → model improvement)"
The data loop Mr. Chesure cares about:
1. Learner plays game → system logs which rounds they get right/wrong
2. Patterns emerge → which concepts are hard, which shortcuts learners try
3. Feedback improves games → rounds get better, distractors get smarter
4. Mr. Chesure validates → every improvement passes pedagogy check

### 4. "How can you do different things other than chatbots?"
Our answer: games, not chatbots. Children don't engage with chatbots.
- Scenario Gate (3-step interactive gate, not conversation)
- Number Frames (manipulative-based game, not chat)
- Building step (creative design, not typing to AI)
- Mr. Chesure is an agent that VALIDATES, not one that TALKS to learners

### 5. "Where is human in the loop?"
- Barbara approves every Learning Contract before building
- Guides approve peer-shared games before other learners play them
- Mr. Chesure + Critic + Inspector verify every game (AI-in-the-loop)
- Barbara tests every game on localhost before shipping

## Pilot results Mr. Chesure should reference
- 11 learners (ages 10-16, Acton Academy Falls Church)
- ALL wanted to keep building after spring break
- Zero prompts needed for creative thinking
- Used math in unexpected, creative ways
- Students naturally grouped up
