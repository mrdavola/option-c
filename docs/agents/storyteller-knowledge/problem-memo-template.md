# Problem Memo Template (from Fellowship)

Source: Michael Raspuzzi's fellowship resources, April 2026.

## Philosophy
"Fall in hate with the problem, not in love with the solution."
— From Google X's culture (spun out Waymo, Wing)

Be steady on the problem. Be flexible on the path to solve it.

## The 5-section memo (< 1 page)

### 1. Solve the problem for one
- Identify ONE real person
- Can be yourself, someone you know, or someone you want to help
- The more real, the easier to get context
- "Do things that don't scale." — Paul Graham

### 2. Problem statement (must be all three)
- **Specific:** scoped starting point, clear root cause (ask "why" 3-5x)
- **Data driven:** quantify how big and important
- **Geographically scoped:** start somewhere specific

Bad: "Kids don't like math"
Good: "200,000 8th graders across the South/Southwest US perform below NAEP average in math. Main causes: chronic absences, fragmented curriculum, poor algebra timing."

### 3. Status quo and the gap
- Top 3-5 solutions your persona currently uses
- Explain the main gap

### 4. Overview of the solution
- Overall idea and why it's better
- Main parts and advantage over status quo

### 5. Who pays, how much, and why
- Who pays (may differ from who benefits — e.g., parents pay for kids)
- How much (benchmark against target customer range)
- Why (utility + emotional reasons)

## Barbara's memo answers
1. **One person:** A guide at Acton Academy Falls Church struggling to make math engaging
2. **Problem:** In traditional education, academic concepts taught in isolation → engagement drops. 11 learners piloted, ALL wanted to keep building after spring break.
3. **Status quo:** Khanmigo (worksheet path), MagicSchool (teacher-only), Synthesis (closed), ChatGPT (no structure)
4. **Solution:** AI-powered math games platform with research-backed pedagogy, 3D galaxy exploration, verified standards alignment
5. **Who pays:** Schools ($250 setup + $19/learner/year) + guides ($29/month). Parents for homeschool.

## Writing guidance
- Human thought and writing FIRST
- Can use LLM to tighten research, but write in own words
- Paul Graham level clarity with Our World in Data level research
- Each section: 2-3 lines max
- Do NOT share AI slop with team
