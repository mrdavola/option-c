# Khanmigo's Death — Why Chatbot Tutors Failed (April 2026)

Source: Dan Meyer, LinkedIn, April 15, 2026. "RIP Khanmigo & Edtech Industry Dreams of AI Tutors"

## Key facts
- Sal Khan admitted: "For a lot of students, it was a non-event. They just didn't use it much."
- Khan Academy's Chief Academic Officer: "So far I am not seeing the revolution in education."
- Students gave "IDK IDK" responses — passive, disengaged interaction
- Khanmigo had to become MORE aggressive (auto-expanding, always-on) because students weren't using it voluntarily
- Khan initially blamed teachers, then blamed students ("students aren't great at asking questions")
- Khan Academy's strongest effect sizes only appeared after excluding 95% of the study population
- Khan now says: "Our biggest lever is really investing in the human systems"

## Why it died
1. Chatbots can't build relationships with students
2. Chatbots can't initiate or end conversations with human sensitivity
3. Students don't naturally engage with text-based AI tutors
4. The technology was pushed onto students who didn't want it
5. Even with massive advantages (OpenAI access, Microsoft backing, government funding, billionaire supporters), it still failed

## What this means for Diagonally
- **We are the anti-Khanmigo.** Kids BUILD, they don't chat.
- Our pilot proved the opposite: "This is fun! They were excited to build games."
- Students naturally grouped up without being told — social learning happened organically
- ALL learners wanted to keep building after spring break
- We invest in human systems: guides, Acton Academy model, peer learning
