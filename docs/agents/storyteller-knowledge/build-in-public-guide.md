# Build in Public Guide (from Worldwide Venture Fellowship)

Source: Michael Raspuzzi's fellowship resources, April 2026.

## Core philosophy
- Don't wait for "perfect" — people follow the process
- If SpaceX can blow up rockets in public, you can share bumps and roadblocks
- Building in public increases surface area of luck — people can't help if they don't know what you're working on

## Three styles
1. **Personal journey:** insights, stories, challenges (Paul Graham style)
2. **Product-led:** demos, user success stories, build insights (Levelsio style)
3. **Founder-led:** company metrics, building insights, demos (David/Jenni AI style)

## Barbara's best fit
Mix of founder-led + personal journey:
- Educator-turned-builder perspective is unique
- Acton Academy network gives credibility
- Fellowship journey provides natural narrative arc
- THE SECRET must be protected (never reveal learners build games)

## What good looks like
- Always have a visual (even just a screenshot)
- Write in your own voice
- Know your audience (parents vs. learners vs. partners)
- Keep it short and sweet

## What to avoid
- Don't post every little update — quality over quantity
- Don't make it a public diary — some thoughts stay internal
- NO AI SLOP — write in your own voice, not with LLMs
- People want to follow Barbara, not Claude

## Exemplar founders referenced
- **David Park (Jenni AI):** Built 3 years, one viral X post → $10M ARR. Founder-led.
- **Vicki Powell (Lume Health):** Posts 1x every 2 weeks with photo + team update. Personal.
- **Levelsio:** One-person product studio. Product-led.
- **Tanay Kothari (Whisper Flow):** Authentic voice, transcribed with AI. Founder-led.
- **Max Brodeur-Urbas (Gumloop):** Great product videos + authentic posts. Founder + product.
- **Paul Graham:** Long-form essays. Personal journey. OG.

## Post frequency
- 2x social posts per week individually (fellowship requirement)
- 1 team blog post per week
- Post in #build-in-public Slack channel
