# Hustle Customer #1 Playbook (from Fellowship)

Source: Michael Raspuzzi's fellowship resources, April 2026.

## Pre-call
1. Build something and demo in a 1-min video
2. Post publicly for credibility OR make custom Loom for that person
3. Must be a shareable, clickable link (Vercel deploy, Claude Artifacts, etc.)

Why build before the call:
- Most customer-facing person should make mockups in 25min
- Can't always ask customers what they want — show it, they respond
- Show > Tell

## During 20min call

### 1. Warm up (5min)
- How's it going with AI? Terrified or on top of it?
- What are their top 3 challenges right now?

### 2. Demo (10min)
- Frame: "As I understand it, [their problem]. So I made this."
- Share screen
- Ask: "What's your first impression?" (3-5min)
  - Get feedback on the problem statement
  - Get feedback on the demo

### 3. Close (5min)
- "I'm starting a 30-day pilot. If this could work for your next 4 weeks, would you pay $20/mo?"
- If yes → boom!
- If no → ask why:
  - Pricing thing?
  - Wrong problem? → "Is there a different problem you'd like help with?"
- It's either not the right problem or not the right person (early adopter)

## After call
- Reply same day with 1 line: "Thank you! I appreciate you sharing that [specific thing]. I'll share an update shortly."
- Within 24hrs send new mockup (20min of work)
- Ask if interested
- Speed wins all deals

## Key insight
"Payment is the most reliable signal of value" — fellowship quote
