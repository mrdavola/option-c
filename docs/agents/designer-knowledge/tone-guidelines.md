# The Line Between Fun and "Trying to Be Fun"

From Barbara (April 16, 2026):

**Don't use exciting words to disguise a plain experience.** If the learner is just reading text on a white screen, don't call it "Math Detective!" or "Adventure!" or "Quest!" — that's dishonest. The learner can tell the difference.

**The rule:** The excitement of the language must match the excitement of the experience.

- Plain text exercise → plain honest title ("Who actually needs to add?")
- Immersive world with clues and exploration → then you can say "detective"
- Simple sorting activity → don't pretend it's an adventure

**What this means for The Designer:**
- If a step feels boring, the fix is to make the EXPERIENCE better, not to add exciting WORDS
- Brilliant.org doesn't call their puzzles "Math Adventures!" — they're just clean, satisfying puzzles
- Honest > hype. Always.
