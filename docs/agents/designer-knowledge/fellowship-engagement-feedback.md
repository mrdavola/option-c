# Fellowship Engagement Feedback for The Designer

Source: Mid-demo feedback and fellowship resources, April 2026.

## Critical questions The Designer must help answer

### "How does token economy work? Gaming has 90% drop-off in first 30min."
This was asked directly by fellowship reviewers. Our engagement design MUST beat the 90% drop-off cliff. Implications:
- First 30 seconds must hook the learner (the 3-second test is real)
- Value must be clear BEFORE any complexity
- Progression must feel earned, not arbitrary
- Token/reward system needs to motivate WITHOUT being the reason to play (intrinsic > extrinsic)

### "How do you ensure quality of UGC competing with DuoLingo (full visual team)?"
DuoLingo has a massive visual design team. We don't. Our advantage:
- Learner ownership (they BUILD, DuoLingo doesn't let you)
- Community (peer games, not corporate content)
- Math IS gameplay (DuoLingo is quiz-wrapper for language)
- But we MUST look polished. Brilliant.org-level clean, not "student project" aesthetic.

## Learner feedback from pilot (11 learners, ages 10-16)
- "Now you've got the VISUALS right" → first reaction to galaxy. Visuals matter.
- Some students don't trust AI to teach them yet → don't over-rely on AI-facing features
- Avoid making it "feel like school" → critical design constraint
- Students naturally grouped up without being told → social features have natural demand
- Used math in unexpected, creative ways → leave room for emergent behavior
- Incorporated environments/topics THEY cared about → personalization drives engagement

## Competitive landscape (design implications)
| Competitor | Their design | Our design advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Khanmigo | Worksheet path | We have games, not worksheets |
| MagicSchool | Teacher dashboard | We're learner-facing |
| Synthesis/Sora | Closed ecosystem | Open, adoptable by any school |
| Generic ChatGPT | Text-only, no structure | Visual, structured, game-based |
| DuoLingo | Polished but quiz-wrapper | Math IS the gameplay |

## Show > Tell (from hustle playbook)
- Build a 1-min demo video for every milestone
- The Designer should flag screenshot-worthy moments
- Every game should be demoable in 60 seconds
- "If you can't demo it, it's not done"

## Key design principle from fellowship
"Speed wins all deals" — fast iteration on mockups (25min turnaround after customer feedback). The Designer should support rapid prototyping, not pixel-perfect planning.
