# K.OA.A.1 — Sample Real-World Scenarios

**Standard:** Represent addition and subtraction with objects, fingers, mental images, drawings, sounds, acting out situations, verbal explanations, expressions, or equations.

**Kid-friendly version:** Add things together to find a total.

---

## Scenario 1 — 💰 Money

**Story:**
You have 4 pennies in your jar. Grandma gives you 3 more pennies for helping her. You count all the pennies in your jar.

**The math:**
4 + 3 = 7

**How to solve it:**
1. Start with the 4 pennies you already have
2. Add the 3 pennies Grandma gave you
3. Count them all together
4. You have 7 pennies

**What 7 means:** The total number of pennies in your jar.

**Game idea it suggests:**
- Title: "Penny Jar"
- Character: Grandma
- Background: kitchen
- Item: coin
- Backstory: "Grandma is helping you count the pennies you saved!"

---

## Scenario 2 — 🐟 Animals

**Story:**
Your fish tank has 6 goldfish swimming around. Today Mom takes you to the pet store and you bring home 2 new fish. Your tank is fuller now.

**The math:**
6 + 2 = 8

**How to solve it:**
1. Start with the 6 fish already in the tank
2. Add the 2 new fish from the store
3. Count all the fish
4. You have 8 fish

**What 8 means:** The total number of fish in your tank.

**Game idea it suggests:**
- Title: "Fish Tank"
- Character: diver
- Background: underwater
- Item: fish
- Backstory: "Catch fish to bring home to your aquarium!"

---

## Scenario 3 — 🚌 Transport

**Story:**
The school bus picks up 5 kids at your stop. At the next stop, 1 more kid hops on. The bus driver counts everyone on the bus.

**The math:**
5 + 1 = 6

**How to solve it:**
1. Start with the 5 kids already on the bus
2. Add the 1 new kid from the next stop
3. Count all the kids on the bus
4. There are 6 kids

**What 6 means:** The total number of kids on the bus.

**Game idea it suggests:**
- Title: "School Bus"
- Character: explorer
- Background: city
- Item: star (as passenger stand-in)
- Backstory: "Drive the bus and pick up all the kids for school!"

---

## Why these specifically

- **All three use objects K kids see daily** (pennies, fish, school bus) — not abstract worlds
- **Numbers stay 1–8** — no overflow, easy to count on fingers
- **Each maps to a clear, instantly-grabbable game theme** (penny jar, fish tank, school bus)
- **Each suggests sprites we already have** in the library so the auto-fill works
- **Stories are 2–3 sentences max** — kindergartners can listen to it read aloud quickly
- **The "game idea" section** is what makes "Use this idea!" auto-fill the builder
